I am a PhD candidate at the School of Earth and Space Sciences, Peking University. My research focuses on Early Triassic astrochronology and paleoclimate modeling, particularly the chronological framework of the Early Triassic, marine and terrestrial stratigraphic correlation, the 1.2 Myr obliquity modulation cycle, astronomically forced paleoclimate change, and CESM-based Earth system modeling. I was a visiting PhD student at the University of Leeds. My work has been published in journals including *Earth and Planetary Science Letters*, *Global and Planetary Change*, and *Science Bulletin*.

## Research Keywords

Early Triassic; cyclostratigraphy; orbital forcing; paleoclimate modeling; paleohydrological change.

## Education

- Sept. 2021 - Jun. 2026, PhD, Peking University.

- Sept. 2017 - Jun. 2021, BSc, Jilin University.

## Research Interests

- Induan astrochronology and marine-terrestrial stratigraphic correlation.
- Orbitally forced paleoclimate change in the Early Triassic.
- CESM-based paleoclimate simulations and hydrological changes in the Early Triassic.
- Continental water storage changes and mechanisms of sea-level response during the Early Triassic hothouse.
- The 1.2 Myr obliquity modulation cycle and its paleoclimatic significance.
